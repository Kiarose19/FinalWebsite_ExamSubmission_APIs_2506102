The Style Guide

Goal Alignment for my website: My goal for this website is to efficiently convey the theme of space and Nasa throughout my website. The reason being I am using a Nasa open API for the data visualisation section of my website. Therefore, the website encapsulates and conveys space as a theme. For example, the use of starry backgrounds solidifies this idea of space, the fun facts, quotes, and jokes related to space and Nasa inform the user that this website is about space and Nasa which in turn also communicates why the data visualisation section makes use of a Nasa API. The target audience/users for this website would most likely be people who are passionate about space and want to know more about the data that Nasa collects.

The Style Process Documentation

Notes on my visual aesthetic: The main visual aesthetic I was trying to achieve for my website as mentioned in the goal alignment section is the one of space. This was mainly done through the background image of each page. The background image is of stars with beautiful colours of orange-yellows, deep blues, and almost greyish white. I chose a busy background because the design throughout the rest of my website is quite simplistic and clean, therefore preventing any clashing/noise that will overwhelm the user by drawing their attention in multiple directions. I specifically chose colours for the textual elements of my website that would enhance the colours of the background while further conveying the theme of space. The colour choices are further discussed in the visual design elements section.

Notes on my visual design elements: Fonts – I have decided to use one font throughout my website to create a cohesive and unified look. I did research on fonts that are most popular and best suited for websites finding a variety of interesting fonts. There were three fonts that I really thought would be suitable for my website. All three fonts had good readability which is something important for websites. However, I decided to choose the Source Sans Pro font. This is because this font was specifically designed for web design making the legibility and readability of high quality. The font was best suited for my website because of the busy background and variation of colours used in my website. Readability and legibility – The font impacts how the user understands the text as well as how convenient the designer makes it for the user to read. My website consists of good readability because the font is clear and concise. My website also makes use of evenly spaced text thereby not overwhelming the user with too much text to read and strain themselves in doing so. Colours – the colour palette that I have decided to use for my website is one that consists of black, blue, yellow, grey, and white. After doing research into modern colour palettes that are popularly used in the website domain, I decided to go with this colour palette. It consists of all the colours that emphasize space alongside working beautifully with the colours from the backgrounds in my website. It helps my website achieve a simple, clean look that feels very modern and inviting. These specific colours are not too harsh on the eye and when used correctly can tie the aesthetic of the website together. The reason these colours work so well together is because most of them are complementary and while the blue and yellow may give the website a pop of colour, the white and grey contrast harmoniously to dim down the busyness that could be created if those colours were used on their own. Hierarchy, Harmony, Balance – Throughout my website I have included the elements that I want the user to see at the top of each page. The use of heading tags has also been helpful in indicating what holds more importance on each page. I have included balance throughout my website by making layouts that ensure the elements of the page are evenly spaced and have similar positions if they have related content. for example, on the blog page the blog posts are the same length and style placed in the same position which indicates a relationship between them but also keeps the page balanced. Shape, Colour, Position - I have implemented rounded rectangles/squares in my website. The rounded effect portrays a friendliness to my website that is not commanding or domineering. It creates ease within the website. The position of elements is placed in a very neat and cohesive way as to not cause confusion in the user of what they mean/represent.
